# Harvey's Math · the funnel that justifies this build

> *"AI doesn't bend the funnel. It bends the input."*

## The funnel (one human Hack · one year)

| Stage | Per week | Per year (× 50 wk) | Conversion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dials | 1,500 (300/day × 5 days) | 75,000 | — |
| Proposals shipped | 8 | 400 | **0.53%** |
| Sits | 3 (range 2-4) | 150 | **37.5%** |
| Rep agreements signed | 1.5 (range 1-2) | 75 | **50%** |
| Listings on market | 1 | 50 | **66%** |
| Closes (× 50% transact) | 0.5 | **25** | **50%** |

**Per-Hack revenue:** 25 closes/year × $90K avg GCI = **$2.25M GCI per Hack/year** · after splits Hack takes home ~$562K/year.

## Two immutable claims

1. **Conversion ratios are physics, not preference.** Donovan refined them over 30 years across multiple market cycles, asset classes, and offices. The funnel is the funnel.
2. **The bottleneck is dial volume, never conversion.** A human Hack tops out at ~300 dials/day.

If you accept those two claims, the Atlas-Bookmaker thesis is forced.

## Where the Bookmaker fits

The Bookmaker covers **stages 2 + 5** of Harvey's funnel:

- **Stage 2: Proposals shipped** (8/week per Hack) — the Bookmaker writes the OM, landing page, e-blast, and CoStar listing for every proposal Harvey ships.
- **Stage 5: Listing on market** — the same creative assets distribute the listing post-rep-agreement.

**Without a Bookmaker, those 8 proposals don't get to market.** That's why the Bookmaker ships before any Hack. The polish team is the upstream dependency for the dial floor.

## At AI scale (same conversion, 100× the dials)

Atlas-Bookmaker doesn't change conversion ratios. It changes the dial budget. If we route dial volume from `Hack-Dialer` (a future Hack-fleet specialist) → `Atlas-Bookmaker` (this repo) → `Hack-Closer` (another future specialist), the same conversion math applies at higher volume.
